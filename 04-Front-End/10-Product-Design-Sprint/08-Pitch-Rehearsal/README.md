## Pitch Rehearsal (30mn)

Time to prepare your pitch! In a few minutes you will be presenting your prototype in front of the class.

But before that, make sure to do **one last clickthrough of your prototype** with a teacher.

For the pitch itself, you should determine:
- Who will be standing in front of the class and pitch 🎤
- Who will be behind the computer and click 👩‍💻

Some recommendations for your presentation:
- **Present as a user**: this is the most efficient way to present your product. Start by presenting your pain as a user, and explain how the app can help address it (e.g. *"Hey my name is Julie, and it's always a struggle to figure out where to stay when I travel to a new city"*)
- **Keep it short**: 3 to 4 minutes should be enough to take us through your Core User Journey.
- **Avoid technical vocabulary**: we want to focus on the user experience, so you should avoid using technical vocabulary (click here, scroll there, etc...) and speaking about future features.
- **Practice your pitch**: the more you practice, the more comfortable you will be and the clearer your presentation will be.

This is the first of many times you will be pitching your product: it will not be perfect, and we know that!

Don't hesitate to take this chance to collect feedback from the class:
- Do they understand your product?
- Do they have suggestions to improve your core journey?

Good luck!
